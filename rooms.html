Preferred Accomodations (ROOMS)



Introduction

At Cambridge Group of Hospitals, the mission is to provide the best healthcare services to people belonging to different socio-economic classes of the country. Keeping this in mind, our staff places great emphasis on patient comfort and care.


The aim is to heal patients while providing them with a relaxing and invigorating space. Therefore, we offer different types of rooms that patients can choose from. The list includes VIP suites, private rooms, semiprivate rooms and the general ward. All spaces have one factor in common


the availability of exceptional doctors, staff and technicians who work in unison to cure any health problem at hand. We want to ensure the stay at Cambridge Hospital not only revives the patient’s health but also empowers them to lead a healthier life.



Choose a room of your preference

At Cambridge Hospital, we offer different wards and rooms for patients based on the privacy and services that make them comfortable. This includes premium suites, the general ward, private rooms, and semiprivate rooms.


Premium Suites

The premium suites aim to make your stay at Cambridge Hospital pleasant and luxurious with special services offered to the patient and their visitors. The area includes the patient’s room, a plush dining area and a comfortable waiting zone for visitors. The room also has a TV, a microwave, a fridge, a sofa set, and a small kitchen. Other special services of the premium room include one dedicated nurse, refreshments such as a fruit basket, dry fruits, soft drinks and water. The meals provided to the patient are based on the items prescribed by the doctor/dietician.

General ward

Since Cambridge Hospital is one of the best private hospital’s in Karachi, the facility’s general ward stays true to the mission of the group by providing quality healthcare services to the patients admitted. The general ward has up to 6-7 beds and includes a shared toilet for the admitted patients. The air-conditioned space has a dedicated staff that checks up on the patients after regular intervals to ensure those admitted are comfortable. The meals served are as prescribed by the doctor/dietician.

Private rooms

With the dimensions of 8×10” & 12×14”, the private rooms at Cambridge Hospital are air-conditioned spaces that have some of the top-notch doctors of Karachi under one roof. The facilities included in the private room are a personal toilet, a TV, microwave, refrigerator and a sofa set. Nurses and staff are available on need basis and also provide regular interval checkups. The meals prescribed to the patients are based on the recommendation of the doctor/dietician.

Semiprivate rooms

The air-conditioned semiprivate rooms are two patient sharing rooms which provide facilities such as a TV, microwave, refrigerator, and a sofa set. Nurses and staff provide regular visits and are also available when otherwise needed. The meals provided to the patients are based on the prescription of the doctor and dietician.



Patient and visitor information

Since the health and comfort of the patients is the topmost priority of the Cambridge Hospital, it is also essential for the hospital to maintain a strict visiting policy to maintain a specific routine of the admitted individuals.



Critical care unit(CCU):

Briefing time by CCU doctors regarding the condition of patients is between 11:00 am to 12:00 pm and 05:00pm to 06:00pm.

RULES FOR ATTENDANT VISITOR:


  1. Only one attendant is allowed to stay with the patient.
  2. Men are not allowed to stay overnight under any circumstances in the women and children semiprivate/general wards.
  3. Smoking, and eating of paan, betel nut, and gutka is not permitted on the hospital premises.
  4. Arms and ammunition are strictly forbidden on the hospital premises. Individuals violating the rule will be prosecuted
  5. Ziauddin Group of Hospitals is strictly against offering gratuities/tips to the staff.
  6. Patients and their attendants are requested not to bring any valuables with them to the hospital. The hospital management is not responsible for the theft or loss of valuables.
  7. When the patient is discharged, the consultant will complete his visit and then complete the patient’s file and send it for billing. It might require some time. In the meanwhile, the patient and the attendees are requested to be tolerant and wait for the procedure to be completed.
  8. The ambulatory service will be available for patients on cash payments.
